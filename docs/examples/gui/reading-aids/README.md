# Reading aids for dyslexia and decoding

star is built for people who work with dense, formatted documents and need help
decoding them. This is a GUI walkthrough of the reading aids — **nothing to run
here**; toggle these in the app while a document is open.

**You'll need:** `star` installed and a document open. Most aids live under
**View ▸ Reading Aids**. Some fetch a small helper package the first time you
turn them on (star offers this automatically).

## The aids

- **Define a word** — put the caret on a word and press **`Ctrl+D`** (or
  **View ▸ Reading Aids ▸ Define Word**) for an offline definition and synonyms.
- **Highlight difficult words** — **`Ctrl+Alt+O`** flags uncommon / academic
  vocabulary so you can pre-teach or look them up.
- **Syllable splitting** — **View ▸ Reading Aids ▸ Syllable Splitting** breaks
  words into syllables (`read·a·bil·i·ty`) as a decoding aid. Display-only:
  speech and highlighting are unaffected.
- **Dyslexia-friendly font** — **`Ctrl+Alt+X`** switches to OpenDyslexic; pick
  other reading fonts under **Preferences ▸ Display ▸ Reading font**.
- **Text spacing** — **`Ctrl+Alt+W`** opens WCAG-style line-height and
  letter/word spacing controls.
- **Reading ruler** and **RSVP one-word mode** — a moving guide bar, or one word
  at a time in the center of the screen, both under **View ▸ Reading Aids**.

## What makes it useful

- Every aid is **transparent to reading**: playback, word highlighting, and
  Define Word keep working with the aid on.
- They combine — e.g. dyslexia font + syllable splitting + difficult-word
  highlighting at once.
